Case Study

I will be talking about our opportunity to redesign the Little Five Points Center for Arts & Community website. For those of you who are not acquainted with the Little Five Points district within Atlanta, it is a vibrant community teeming with artistic flair and musically-inclined individuals. The Little Five Points Center, either known as The Center or L5PCAC, is a non profit whose mission is to house smaller, local nonprofits within the closed down Moreland Elementary School. They take nonprofits under their wing, so to speak, to allow them to thrive.

Meet Lekesha (Proto-Persona)

In comes Lakesha Brian, a middle-age fitness instructor with a young family. She worked at a gym teaching classes until the pandemic hit and, because the gym closed down, she is now looking for a reasonably priced space to host her own workout sessions. She wants a place with youth activities, a sound system, and tables and chairs for her clients to relax between classes. 

User Research & Competition

For our user research, we sent out a Google Form with questions asking what people look for in community centers and how they find out information about them when booking events. We discovered users want places with amenities, such as lighting, sound systems, childcare, and, in this day and age, a ready-made protocol for COVID. With this information in tow, we conducted 1:1 interviews, both with potential users and with the Center itself. Users found the website to be difficult to navigate and find information. One user mentioned how the website was like an edgy teenagers, early 2000s, website on the dark web.

Current competitors are other community centers near Atlanta.

Problem Statement

Little 5 Points Center for Arts & Community (L5PCAC)’s website was designed to provide information about the community, rental services within their facilities, and tenant partnerships. We have observed that our product is not representing itself, the community, nor its mission, which is causing users to overlook its services. How might we improve the L5PCAC web presence so that users are more likely to use the community center based on return visits to the site, increased donations, and increased financial gains through property rentals?

Styl Tile & Logo Redesign

When developing our Style Tile, our color palate was largely inspired by the murals that can be seen around the Little Five Points area.
We were also asked to maintain the colors in their existing logo.
We ended up with a Split Complementary color palate of a yellow/gold, burgundy, peach, and teal.

We wanted the site aesthetic to reflect both the graffiti art in the area, and the art programs the Center houses.
For example, the navigation hover feature is a paint stroke.

If you didn’t know, Little Five Points gets its name from the literal FIVE points that intersect at the center of the district.
We paid tribute to that through the use of 5 images in our carousels, and the 5-pointed star icon (which is also carried over from their previous logo)
While we all brainstormed and collaborated on the new logo design

Logo Final Design

we began our redesign process for web and mobile. We each did sketches to come up with an idea of how the homepage should be laid out. The drawing you see is the layout we felt would best suit the needs asked for by little five from paper to hi fidelity. After the homepage was laid out, we started A/B testing to make iterations to the navigation and site pages: on the desktop, we changed around the the navigation to make it flow better. Also, we changed “rental” to “rental space” and “partners” to “our partners”.
